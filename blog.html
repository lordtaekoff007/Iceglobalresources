The legendary BlaqHawk5.5 Mercedes Benz E550 V8 Engine now up for sale for just 15.8M

The Mercedes-Benz E550 is a popular model within the E-Class lineup, known for its luxury, performance, and the power of its V8 engine. Here are some key facts about the E550 V8 engine


Engine Specifications: Engine Type: 4.6-liter V8 Twin-Turbocharged Power Output: Approximately 402 horsepower (HP) at 5,000-5,750 RPM Torque: 443 lb-ft at 1,600-4,750 RPM Fuel Injection: Direct fuel injection (DFI) for improved efficiency and power


Performance: 0-60 MPH Time: Around 4.9 seconds, depending on the model year and configuration. Top Speed: Electronically limited to 130 MPH (standard in most configurations). Transmission: Paired with a 7-speed automatic transmission (7G-Tronic) or later a 9-speed automatic transmission (9G-Tronic)


Efficiency: Fuel Economy: The E550 V8 averages around 17-18 MPG in the city and 24-26 MPG on the highway, depending on the model year and driving conditions. Technology: Incorporates turbocharging, direct injection, and start-stop systems to enhance fuel efficiency without sacrificing performance


Models and Variants: The E550 V8 engine has been used in various body styles, including the sedan, coupe, and convertible (Cabriolet). Available with rear-wheel drive (RWD) or Mercedes' 4MATIC all-wheel-drive (AWD) system, offering versatility in different driving conditions


Engineering and Design: Aluminum Block and Heads: The engine uses lightweight materials to improve performance and efficiency. Turbochargers: Two turbochargers are used to provide a balance of power and responsiveness. Cooling System: Features an advanced cooling system to manage the high output of the engine, especially under performance conditions


Sound and Exhaust: The V8 engine in the E550 is known for its deep, throaty exhaust note, which enhances the overall driving experience


Legacy: The E550 V8 engine has been praised for its combination of luxury, power, and refinement, making it a favorite among enthusiasts who value performance in a luxury sedan. It’s one of the last V8s offered in the E-Class before the lineup moved towards smaller turbocharged engines and hybrid powertrains
